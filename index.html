🛒 Materials You'll Need

🧽 Fun foam sheets (craft foam)
🖊️ Ballpoint pen (NOT felt tip)
🎨 Ink pads (different colours)
📄 Plain paper (for printing)
✂️ Scissors (to cut foam)
📱 Phone/scanner (to digitise)

How to Make Your Prints

1
Prepare the Foam
  • Cut the foam to the size you want
  • Clean it to remove any dust
  • Make sure the surface is flat and smooth
2
Draw Your Design

Use a ballpoint pen to draw your design on the foam.

How Foam Printing Works

➖

Pressed Lines
Where you draw = NO ink

⬛

Raised Areas
Flat foam = INK prints

  • Press hard to make deep lines in the foam
  • The lines you draw will NOT have ink
  • The flat parts WILL have ink
  • Think in reverse — draw what you DON'T want printed
3
Add Ink
  • Press the ink pad onto the foam
  • Make sure all the flat parts have ink
  • The lines you drew should have no ink in them
  • Don't use too much ink — it can fill in your lines
4
Print on Paper
  • Put the foam face down on paper
  • Press down evenly on the back
  • Don't move the foam while pressing
  • Lift up carefully to see your print
5
Experiment!

Make at least 3 different prints. Try:

  • Different patterns — geometric, organic, abstract
  • Different colours — try multiple ink pad colours
  • Layering — print on top of previous prints
  • Partial prints — don't always use the whole stamp
⚠️

Important: Use a Ballpoint Pen!

You MUST use a ballpoint pen to draw on the foam.

  • DO NOT use felt tip pens — they don't make lines in the foam
  • DO NOT use pencils — too soft, won't work
  • Press hard enough to make a visible indent
  • If the lines are too shallow, they will fill with ink
💡

Tips for Great Prints

  • Simple designs work best — don't make lines too close together
  • Press evenly — uneven pressure = patchy prints
  • Let ink dry before layering another colour
  • Clean your stamp with a damp cloth between colours
  • Make extras — some prints will be better than others
  • Keep your foam stamps — you can reuse them!

💭 Think About Your Designs

Before you start, consider:

  • What patterns would work well for your project?
  • Do you want regular repeating patterns or random organic shapes?
  • What colours fit your design theme?
  • How might you combine multiple prints together?
  • Could you create a set of stamps that work together?
